10. jelaskan maksud dari listening,speaking,reading,writing


Empat komponen itu biasanya disebut dengan language skills.
Listening: a skill to listen utterance(s) in English
Speaking: a skill to speak in English
Reading: a skill to read
Writing: a skill to write in English correctly
Generally, when we start to learn a new language, we usually learn to listen first, then to speak, after that to read, and finally to write.

18. Design a good critical reading text and critical writing text!


1.Previewing:Learning about a text before really reading it.
2.Contextualizing:Placing a text in its historical, biographical, and cultural contexts.
3.Questioning to understand and remember:Asking questions about the content.
4.Reflecting on challenges to your beliefs and values:Examining your personal responses.
5.Outlining and summarizing:Identifying the main ideas and restating them in your own words.
6.Evaluating an argument:Testing the logic of a text as well as its credibility and emotional impact.
7.Comparing and contrasting related readings:Exploring likenesses and differences between texts to understand them better.

34. What are differences between imitative speaking and imitative writing, intensive reading and intensive speaking, and responsive listening and extensive speaking?


Imitative speaking is simply the ability to parrot back a word or phrase or a sentence.
Examples include directed response tasks, reading aloud, sentence and dialogue completion, limited picture-cued tasks.

Imitative writing requires students to demonstrate skills in the fundamental tasks of writing letters, correctly spelling words, correctly placing punctuation marks, and constructing very brief sentences

Intensive reading is focuses on closely following a shorter text, doing exercises with it, and learning it in detail. According to this approach, this helps language learners really understand the language’s grammar and syntax.

Intensive speaking involves producing a limit amount of language in a highly control context. An example of this would be to read aloud a passage or give a direct response to a simple question.

Responsive listening involves listening to a small amount of a language such as a command, question, or greeting. After listening, the student is expected to develop an appropriate short response.

Extensive speaking is normal some sort of monolog.
Examples include speech, story-telling, etc. This involves a great deal of preparation and is not typically improvisational communication.
