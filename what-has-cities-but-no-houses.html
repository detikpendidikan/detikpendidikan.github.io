Degree of Comparison digunakan untuk menyatakan tingkat perbandingan dari suatu objek/benda atau manusia.

Degree of Comparison terbagi atas tiga, yaitu :

1. Positive

=> membandingkan dua objek di tingkat yang sama

2. Comparative

=> membandingkan dua objek di tingkat yang berbeda

3. Superlative

=> membandingkan satu objek dari keseluruhan

Rumus Degree of Comparison :

1. Positive

- S1 + to be + as + Adjective + as + S2

- S1 + Verb + as + Adverb + as + S2 + Verb

2. Comparative

- S1 + to be + Adjective + -er + than + S2

- S1 + to be + more + Adjective + than + S2

- S1 + Verb + Adverb + -er + than + S2 + Verb

- S1 + Verb + more + Adverb + than + S2 + Verb

3. Superlative

- Subject + to be + the + Adjective + -est + Object

- Subject + to be + the + most + Adjective + Object

- Subject + Verb + the + Adverb + -est + Object

- Subject + Verb + the + most + Adverb + Object

34. Change the following sentence into passive"The storm has damaged a lot of houses"Select one:a. A lot of houses have been damaged by the storm.b. A lot of houses have damaged by the stormc. A lot of houses has damaged by the stormd. A lot of houses has been damaged by the stormtolong bantu manteman kakak Kaka adek adek bapak bapak ibu ibu​


a. A lot of houses have been damaged by the storm.

EXPLANATION

Passive voice is used to focus on the recipient of the verb and the action rather than the doer of action. The main construction of passive voice is the combination of auxiliary BE and the past participle.

The steps how to change the active voice to the passive voice are

~ the object of active voice is moved to the subject of passive voice. Meanwhile, the subject of active voice is moved to the object of passive voice accompanied with the "by" preposition or sometimes it's omitted.

~ all of the verbs are changed into the past participle (V³).

BASED ON YOUR QUESTION

Present perfect

A : S+has/have+V3+O

P : S(O)+has/have+been+V3+by+Agent

Subject (The storm)+Verb(has damaged)+Object(a lot of houses).

changes to :

New subject (A lot of houses)+Verb(have been damaged)+by+Agent (the storm).

➡ A lot of houses is categorized in the plural form. Thus, aux. verb "have" is used.
